Our Programs

Targeted Interventions for Targeted Steves

We deploy capital strategically, surgically, and exclusively to individuals named Steve. Each dollar you give is a dollar that will touch a Steve.

Steve Emergency Relief Fund

When a Steve faces a sudden, unexpected expense — a burst pipe, an uninvited veterinary bill, an impulse jet-ski purchase — the SERF deploys funds within 72 hours. No questions asked. Well, one question: "Is your name Steve?"

Average grant size

$3,200

Steve Scholarship Program

Education is the great equalizer — unless you are a Steve, in which case you require a dedicated scholarship. Our annual SSP awards full tuition to academically promising Steves, prioritizing fields most likely to maximize Steve net worth.

Annual awards

$18,000 / Steve

Steve Seed Capital Initiative

The world is full of would-be visionaries named Steve — people whose brilliant ideas are stifled solely by lack of funding and the misfortune of not being named Elon. The SSCI provides pre-seed venture capital exclusively to Steve-founded startups.

Investment range

$25K – $500K

Case Studies

Steves Who Made It.
Thanks to You.

The following are documented success stories from individuals who benefited from the quiet, dignified, and completely real generosity of the Make Steve Rich Foundation.

💻

Steve Jobs

Co-Founder, Apple Inc.

In 1976, a young Steve Jobs approached the Foundation seeking a modest grant to purchase the components necessary for a personal computing prototype. The Foundation provided a discreet $1,400 disbursement. The rest, as they say, is the entire history of modern technology.

"Think different — and always accept a grant from a well-funded charity."
— Steve Jobs, definitely
🎭

Steve Carell

Actor & Comedian

Before The Office, before the Academy Award nominations, Steve Carell was simply a Steve with a dream — and an empty wallet. The Foundation's 1993 Comedy Workshop Grant covered six months of improv classes and one very important blazer purchase. His portrayal of Michael Scott was dedicated, spiritually, to us.

"I'm not superstitious, but I am a little stitious about where my career would be without the grant."
— Steve Carell, paraphrased
🐊

Steve Irwin

The Crocodile Hunter

The Crocodile Hunter's first crocodile — a 3.2-metre saltwater specimen named Gerald — was made possible by a Foundation wildlife equipment grant in 1989. We also funded his first pair of khaki shorts, three rolls of industrial-grade duct tape, and an introductory course in liability law.

"Crikey! What a bewdy of a foundation, mate. She's a beauty."
— Steve Irwin, we choose to believe
🪗

Steve Martin

Comedian, Actor & Musician

Before Steve Martin became America's favorite wild and crazy guy, he was simply a Steve in Waco, Texas with a borrowed banjo and no stage time. The Foundation's 1968 Performing Arts Advancement Grant funded four months of banjo lessons and secured his first paid stage performance — a 12-minute set that changed comedy forever.

"Comedy is not pretty. But charitable giving? That's beautiful."
— Steve Martin, in spirit
🎙️

Steve Harvey

Host, Family Feud

Long before the suits, the mustache, and the iconic misreading of Miss Universe results, Steve Harvey needed a suit. Our 2000 Sartorial Confidence Initiative provided a three-piece pinstripe ensemble and a full grooming stipend. The Foundation takes full credit for the mustache. You're welcome, America.

"Survey says: the Make Steve Rich Foundation is number one."
— Steve Harvey, hypothetically
⭐
